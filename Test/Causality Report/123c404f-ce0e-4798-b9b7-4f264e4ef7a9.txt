Causal Exploration: The identified cardiomegaly indicates an enlarged heart, which can contribute to congestive heart failure (CHF) and is often associated with pulmonary vascular redistribution and pleural effusions. Bilateral pleural effusions are caused by the accumulation of excess fluid in the pleural space, often secondary to CHF.
