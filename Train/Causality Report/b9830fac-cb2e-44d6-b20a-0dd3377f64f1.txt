However, the findings of the chest X-ray demonstrate that the lungs are normally expanded and clear, and there is no pleural effusion or pneumothorax. These radiographic findings suggest that there is no acute cardiopulmonary abnormality present, thereby ruling out the suspected pulmonary edema as a cause for the chest pain.
