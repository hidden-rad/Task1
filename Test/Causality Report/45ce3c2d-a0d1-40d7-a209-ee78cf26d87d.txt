Causal Exploration: The indication for the chest examination was to assess for any cardiopulmonary abnormalities in a patient with HCC and Cirrhosis. The findings revealed a normal heart, lungs, hila, mediastinum, and pleural surfaces, indicating that there are no cardiopulmonary abnormalities despite the underlying conditions of HCC and Cirrhosis. This suggests that the hepatocellular carcinoma and cirrhosis have not affected the cardiopulmonary system.
